What is Hijama?

Hijama—also known as cupping—is a traditional therapy that uses cups to create gentle suction on the skin. It is practiced in many cultures and referenced in Islamic tradition. People often seek it for general wellness, relaxation, or as part of their personal self-care routines.

Evidence for health benefits varies, and responses differ from person to person. Always consult a licensed healthcare professional for diagnosis or treatment of medical conditions.

What to Expect in a Session
  1. Intake & Consent: brief discussion of goals, history, and any concerns.
  2. Preparation: modesty-first setup, skin cleaning, cup placement plan.
  3. Cupping: cups applied to create gentle suction for a short, timed interval.
  4. Wrap-up: cup removal, aftercare guidance, and follow-up recommendations.
Aftercare (General)
  • Keep areas clean and dry for the rest of the day
  • Hydrate well and rest if needed
  • Temporary marks are common and fade with time
Important Considerations
  • Not suitable for everyone (e.g., certain skin conditions, bleeding risks, pregnancy areas, etc.)
  • Consult a licensed healthcare professional for medical advice or if you have health concerns
  • Hijama services provided are wellness-oriented and non-diagnostic
