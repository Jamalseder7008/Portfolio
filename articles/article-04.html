The Purpose of Using Technology

September 28, 2025 ~7–9 min read

Our purpose in using technology is to advance good and prevent harm. Technology—tools and systems born from applied science—can become a catalyst for truth when guided by sound ethics and clear intention. If evidence and reasoning are our standards, then truth must remain our specification throughout its use.

A healthy tech culture starts with a foundation of discourse: communities and classrooms where ideas are weighed by their truth, not merely by who speaks them. We value evidence over ego, refutation over flattery, universality over convenience. I am here to advise and be advised—encourage me when I am right; counsel me when I am wrong.

Speaking Truth, Restraining Falsehood

“Enjoin what is right and forbid what is wrong” (Qur’an 3:104) frames online conduct as moral work. In practice: publish clearly; verify sources; avoid spreading what we don’t know; correct errors openly. Even private systems demand integrity—truthfulness honors the One who sees all, whether or not people do.

Relationships, Community & the ‘Digital Divide’

Communication tech has transformed relationships: letters became emails; voice and video bridge oceans. Used well, these tools strengthen families, seed opportunities, and protect meaning across distance. Yet entire regions still lack access—the Digital Divide. Our response should be a Digital Unification: equitable access, training, and safeguards so communities can pursue knowledge and growth, not exploitation.

Governance, Justice & Shared Good

Institutions carry a duty to oppose oppression and promote justice. Technology should aid that mission—cooperation on safety standards, secure communication, and open knowledge that helps people flourish. Tech must neither be autocratic nor anarchic; it should balance individual dignity with communal well-being.

Charity, Integrity & Security

Spreading beneficial knowledge is a form of charity. Tools like Quran.com make learning widely accessible—may Allah reward those builders. Conversely, theft and abuse in tech multiply harms: they deprive people of resources that could have become charity and corrode trust. We must design against misuse and speak against oppression online and offline.

Technology that Multiplies Benefit

Consider aviation: multiple layers of technology—airframes, navigation, scheduling, online ticketing—enable family reunions, pilgrimages, commerce, and service. Properly guided, such systems amplify good through society.

Education First

A thriving civilization invests in education. Teachers and students need reliable hardware, fair access, and honest software. Coercion produces resistance; invitation produces mastery. When students choose to learn, they become the next foundation of discourse—maintaining truth, improving tools, and building wisely.

Patience, Intention, and Growth

Good actions multiply: “Whoever brings a good deed will have ten like it…” (Qur’an 6:160). In tech, each truthful contribution can ripple through countless users. Our task is to keep intention sincere, correct errors patiently, and protect communities from misinformation. Let wisdom water this tree of technology so its fruit benefits all.

May we build programs useful in this world and desired in the next; promote justice; forbid harm; and persist with patience.
