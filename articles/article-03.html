Online Audit Scheduler

September 28, 2025 ~3 min read

To schedule an event responsibly, we must find a time that avoids conflicts with other events and present the result so users can quickly see what matters. This is a classic constraint satisfaction problem (CSP). Our team set out to build a working prototype for scheduling audits at our university—simple enough for professors to use, yet smart enough to respect real-world constraints.

The system spans frontend, backend, and AI layers to deliver a full-stack experience: clean forms and calendars for users, reliable storage and APIs for data, and a scheduler that uses constraints to find feasible times.

Approach

  • Model: Courses, rooms, instructors, and time slots with constraints (availability, capacity, precedence).
  • Search: Backtracking with heuristics; forward checking to prune early.
  • UX: Conflict hints inline; calendar views; one-click reschedule suggestions.
  • Data: REST endpoints; normalized tables for audits, rooms, users, and assignments.
